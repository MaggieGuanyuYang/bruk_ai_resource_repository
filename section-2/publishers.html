What publishers said about AI use

Summaries of what major academic publishers currently require when authors, editors, or reviewers use AI. Always check the publisher's most current policy before submission. Last updated: 16 April 2025.

On this page

  • Nature Portfolio / Springer Nature
  • Elsevier
  • Taylor & Francis
  • SAGE
  • Wiley
  • APA Publications
  • Frontiers
  • Cambridge University Press
  • PNAS
  • MDPI
  • Cell Press

Nature Portfolio / Springer Nature

Official policy

AI authorship

  • LLMs don't meet authorship criteria; cannot be attributed as authors.
  • Must document LLM use in Methods section.
  • "AI-assisted copy editing" (grammar/style improvements) doesn't require disclosure.
  • Human accountability required for final text.

Generative AI images

  • Strictly prohibits generative AI images in publications.
  • Exceptions: licensed agency images, AI-specific research pieces, verified scientific tools.
  • All exceptions must be labelled as AI-generated.

AI use by peer reviewers

  • Peer reviewers shouldn't upload manuscripts into generative AI tools.
  • If AI supported evaluation, must declare transparently in review report.

Editorial use

  • Springer Nature uses internal AI tools for accessory content (summaries, glossaries).
  • All content edited and fact-checked by humans.

Elsevier

Official policy

Writing process

  • AI acceptable only for readability/language improvement.
  • Requires human oversight and careful review.
  • Authors must disclose AI use in manuscript.
  • Authors remain fully accountable.

Figures, images, artwork

  • Prohibits generative AI for creating/altering images.
  • Exception: AI as part of research methodology (must describe in Methods).
  • No generative AI for graphical abstracts.

Taylor & Francis

Official policy

For authors

Supports responsible AI use for idea generation, language improvement, interactive search, literature classification, and coding assistance.

Prohibitions:

  • Cannot list AI as author.
  • Cannot replace core researcher responsibilities.
  • No synthetic data generation without robust methodology.
  • No generative AI for images/figures.

Disclosure: Must acknowledge AI tool name, version, usage method, and reason. Statement in Methods or Acknowledgments section.

For editors & peer reviewers

  • Must not upload unpublished manuscripts into generative AI tools.
  • Protects confidentiality and intellectual property.

SAGE

Official policy

Tool types

  • Assistive AI: Makes suggestions to author-created content (no disclosure required).
  • Generative AI: Produces original content (disclosure mandatory).

Disclosure

  • Must reveal all AI-generated content.
  • Detail where content appears.
  • Provide disclosure template with submission.

Key considerations

  • Verify accuracy of AI-generated content.
  • Check sources and citations carefully.
  • Appropriate citation of AI-generated material required.
  • Avoid plagiarism and copyright infringement.
  • Be aware of bias in AI outputs.

Prohibited uses

  • Don't create/modify core research data with AI.
  • Never share sensitive information on AI platforms.
  • Editors/reviewers must maintain peer review confidentiality.

Wiley

Official policy

General guidance

  • Welcomes thoughtful AI use.
  • Authors remain fully accountable.
  • Must review AI tool terms and conditions.
  • Human oversight essential; AI cannot replace writing process.

Disclosure

  • Document all AI technology use (purpose, impact, review process).
  • Disclose upon submission.
  • Maintain documentation.

Peer review

  • GenAI can improve written feedback only.
  • Must transparently declare this use.
  • Editors/reviewers must not upload manuscript portions into AI tools.

APA Publications

Official policy

  • Disclose AI use in Methods section.
  • Cite the AI tool used.
  • Cannot name AI as author.
  • Authors must verify accuracy of all information and citations.
  • Must upload full AI output as supplemental material.

Privacy note: Data entered into generative AI is accessible to the operating organisation. Authors should consider participant privacy implications.

Frontiers

Official policy

AI-generated text

  • Must disclose in Acknowledgments if used to generate main text.
  • Authors responsible for factual accuracy.
  • Cannot list AI as co-author.

Figures and images

  • Must verify accuracy and reflect data correctly.
  • Check for plagiarism.
  • Acknowledge in Acknowledgments and Methods sections.
  • List tool name, version, model, and source.
  • Encouraged to upload all prompts/outputs as supplementary files.

Editors and reviewers

  • Must not use generative AI to review manuscript content.
  • Responsibility for integrity must remain with human experts.

Cambridge University Press

Official policy

  • AI use must be declared and clearly explained.
  • Cannot be listed as author (doesn't meet accountability requirements).
  • Authors accountable for accuracy, integrity, and originality.
  • Use must not breach plagiarism policy.

Individual journals may have more specific requirements.

PNAS (Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences)

Official policy

  • Disclose generative AI use in Materials and Methods (or Acknowledgments).
  • Cannot list AI as author.
  • Authors solely accountable; must thoroughly fact-check outputs.
  • AI tools for creating images/graphics prohibited unless subject of research.
  • No AI in cover art submissions.

MDPI

Official policy (Updated 20 April 2023)

  • AI cannot be listed as author.
  • Authors fully responsible for originality, validity, and integrity.
  • Must declare AI tool use when submitting.
  • Provide transparency in Acknowledgments section.
  • Describe detailed usage in Materials and Methods section.
  • Aligns with COPE position statement.

Cell Press

Official policy

Writing process

  • AI acceptable only for improving readability and language.
  • Must apply with human oversight and control.
  • Authors must carefully review and edit results.
  • Authors ultimately responsible and accountable.

Disclosure statement

  • Required declaration at manuscript submission.
  • Create new section after "declaration of interests".
  • Must include tool name, reason for use, statement of author responsibility.
  • Doesn't apply to basic tools (grammar, spelling, reference checking).

Figures, images, artwork

Follows Elsevier policy (no generative AI except as research methodology).

Peer review process

  • Currently prohibits generative AI use by reviewers/editors.
  • Actively evaluating compliant AI tools for future policy revision.
